Mes Amis et Moi

What is this site?

From February 15th to February 24th, I got to travel to France and Spain. Anika, Aimee, Emily,
Josh, Kyra, Sarah, and I spent our winter break traveling with students at another high school in the
district. I felt really lucky I got to go on this trip (special thank you to my mom), especially since going
with my friends made it an even more incredible and fun experience. I created this site so I could write
about the memories we made each day, what we saw and learned, and so I could post these pictures.

Where exactly did we go?

We went to Paris, Versailles, Nice, and Monaco. Then, we went to Provence with a stop in the pastoral town Vincent Van Gogh found artistic inspiration, Arles. Next, we went to the medieval Carcassonne, a town built by the Visigoths. Finally, we ended our trip in Barcelona, Spain.

What are my goals for this project?

I first had the idea to do my travel journal electronically, because my original jounal was only about 60 small pages. I had too many pictures and too much to write for everything to fit in that tiny book (which I think I will just use for layer photos and postcards). Not only do I want to make a journal, but I want to make a project that I would be proud to show people. I want to be able to share my experiences as well as gain skills in coding that I would be able to showcase. I'm going to do my best to refrain from using any templates, so I can fully understand what's going on in my code and get to relearn the languages. Structure wise, I want to make this a bit more creative than a typical blog. For the home page I've already done the fixed background scrolling set up (which I love!), and I'm so happy to have learned how to do it and how it works. For the other webpages, the idea I have right now (though it may change) is to have them be collections of "postcards." These postcards will be pictures I took that can be flipped over to reveal journal notes on the back. I'm not sure how well everything will fit, so I may have a section at the end of each page with extra pictures and/or notes. I have a lot of learning and coding to do!(But I'm still very excited!!)
